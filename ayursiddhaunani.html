Unani medicine has its origins in ancient Greece and was developed further by Arab and Persian scholars. It is based on the concept of the four humors (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile) and focuses on maintaining their balance for good health.

Unani medicine, also known as Unani Tibb, has its origins in ancient Greece (Yunani), and it was later enriched by Arab and Persian scholars. The system is based on the teachings of Hippocrates and Galen, and it reached India through Arab traders and scholars. Unani medicine is based on the balance of four humors: blood (Dam), phlegm (Balgham), yellow bile (Safra), and black bile (Sauda). It focuses on maintaining this balance for overall health and treats illness by using natural substances such as herbs, minerals, animal products, as well as regimental therapies like cupping, massage, and detoxification. Unani medicine emphasizes prevention, proper diet (Ilaj Bil Ghiza), lifestyle adjustments, and strengthening the bodyтАЩs natural healing ability. Today, it is a recognized system of medicine in India and is widely used for chronic conditions, digestive issues, and general wellness.

Siddha is one of the oldest systems of medicine, primarily practiced in South India. It focuses on balancing the three humors (Vata, Pitta, and Kapha) and uses a unique combination of herbs, minerals, metals, and yoga for treatment and rejuvenation.

The Siddha system of medicine is one of the oldest medical traditions in the world, originating from ancient Tamil civilization. It is believed to have been developed by 18 great Siddhars, with Sage Agathiyar considered the father of Siddha medicine. The word тАЬSiddhaтАЭ means тАЬone who has attained perfection.тАЭ Siddha medicine is based on the five elements тАФ earth, water, fire, air, and space тАФ and focuses on balancing the three vital energies (Vatha, Pitha, and Kapha) in the body. Siddha medicine is not only curative but also preventive, with special emphasis on rejuvenation (Kayakalpa) and longevity.
